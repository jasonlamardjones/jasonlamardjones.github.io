## Project Context
These are promotional materials for an American Space Mindelo / UniCV America250 initiative titled “Independence, Opportunity & the Future.” The goal is to promote workshops, resource discovery, English/culture activities, eLibraryUSA access support, and final-week events in a public-safe, bilingual English/Portuguese format.

The materials should feel visually strong, polished, patriotic, inviting, student-friendly, and credible. They should preserve the rich poster style: dark navy background, red/blue/gold palette, fireworks/stars, Mindelo waterfront/civic visual motifs, clear icons, QR call-to-action, and bilingual English/Portuguese content.

## Non-Negotiable Parameters
Check each flyer against these requirements:

### Naming and branding
- Use **American Space Mindelo** singular.
- Do **not** use “American Spaces Mindelo.”
- Logo/header treatment should be consistent across the set: American Space Mindelo + UniCV / Universidade Cabo Verde + FCSHA.
- Flag any flyer whose logo/header appears inconsistent, malformed, misspelled, low-resolution, or visually mismatched.

### Location
Preferred location language:
- English: **Location: American Space Mindelo / UniCV**
- Portuguese: **Local: American Space Mindelo / UniCV**

Flag any inconsistent location wording, including “UniCV / American Space Mindelo,” “American Space Mindelo / UniCV” without the label, or malformed text.

### Time
Preferred time language:
- English: **Time to be announced**
- Portuguese: **Horário a anunciar**

Flag any typo, spacing issue, or nonstandard wording.

### QR CTA
Preferred CTA:
- English: **Scan for registration, schedule updates & details**
- Portuguese: **Digitalize para inscrição, horários e detalhes**

Flag any typos such as “regstration,” “schudle,” “detals,” awkward Portuguese, line breaks that harm clarity, or duplicated/conflicting QR instructions.

### Website / Resource Hub boundary
- Visible website URLs should not appear on the flyers.
- The QR can point to registration/schedule/details later, but the visible flyer should not advertise Jason’s website.
- Do not imply the Resource Hub is an official Embassy, American Spaces, or UniCV site.

### July 4 / final-week restrictions
- Do not list Djan Djan, BBQ, alcohol, café, or private July 4 venue details publicly.
- July 4 should remain invite-only or “details shared with registered participants.”
- No public claim of official Embassy approval unless explicitly confirmed.

### eLibraryUSA wording
Use cautious wording unless confirmed by the coordinator:
- Safer: “Attend the workshop and learn how to register for eLibraryUSA access.”
- Avoid promising guaranteed account access unless the process is approved.
- Flag any wording that overpromises eLibraryUSA access, certificates, or account activation.

### Workshop names
Expected public workshop structure:
- Workshop 1: **What Do I Want to Build?**
- Workshop 2: **Find Your Next Step**
- Workshop 3: **Three Pathways to Opportunity**
- Workshop 4 / Special Workshop: **eLibraryUSA Access Workshop**

### Workshop 2 correction
Workshop 2 must keep **Free Resources**, not be changed to “Learning Pathways.”
The icon should suggest reading/books/resources, not prizes or giveaways.

## Translation Review Requirements
Review all Portuguese copy for:
- grammar;
- naturalness for Cabo Verde/Portuguese-speaking public context;
- whether the meaning accurately matches the English;
- tone: clear, warm, not bureaucratic;
- spelling/accents;
- awkward literal translations;
- incorrect capitalization;
- inconsistent translation of recurring phrases.

For every translation issue, provide:
1. current text if readable;
2. problem;
3. recommended replacement text.

Do not create a whole new translation system unless necessary. Keep the existing bilingual style where it works.

## Promotional Effectiveness Review
For each flyer, rate the following on a 1–10 scale:
- visual impact / stopping power;
- clarity of the offer;
- credibility / institutional polish;
- bilingual accessibility;
- likely appeal to students and young adults in Mindelo/São Vicente;
- QR/action clarity;
- overall public-readiness.

Then give:
- one paragraph on expected public reception;
- the strongest element;
- the weakest element;
- the top 3 fixes before public release.

## Review Output Format
Use this structure:

### 1. Executive Verdict
- Overall package rating: __/10
- Public-readiness status: Ready / Needs minor fixes / Needs major fixes / Not ready
- Main reason

### 2. Critical Issues Table
Columns:
- Severity: Critical / High / Medium / Low
- Flyer
- Issue
- Why it matters
- Exact recommended fix

### 3. Per-Flyer Review
For each flyer:
- Scorecard table with the rating categories above
- Compliance notes
- Translation notes
- Promotional effectiveness notes
- Exact text fixes needed

### 4. Translation Replacement Bank
Provide a clean list of recommended Portuguese replacements for recurring phrases and flyer-specific text.

### 5. Final Recommendation
State whether the designer should:
- publish as-is;
- make minor text/logo fixes only;
- revise specific flyers;
- rebuild any flyer;
- request coordinator confirmation before release.

## Important Constraint
Do not overfocus on tiny aesthetic preferences if the materials are already strong. Prioritize issues that affect public readiness: logo consistency, typos, translation quality, QR clarity, official-boundary safety, July 4 privacy, eLibraryUSA claim accuracy, and whether the flyers will actually motivate attendance.
